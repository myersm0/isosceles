THE THREE FUNCTIONS OF "QUE" IN FRENCH:

1. PRON (relative pronoun) — refers back to a nominal antecedent:
   "le livre que j'ai lu" — que = PRON (refers to livre, object of lu)
   "l'homme que j'ai vu" — que = PRON (refers to homme, object of vu)
   "C'est Jean que j'ai vu" — que = PRON (cleft, refers to Jean)
   Key signal: que functions as the object of the verb in its clause, \
and there is a noun before it that it refers to.

2. SCONJ (subordinating conjunction) — introduces a clause:
   Completive: "je veux que tu viennes" — after verbs of \
wanting/thinking/saying
   "il savait que les poissons..." — after savoir
   "disant que nous étions..." — after dire (including participles)
   "persuadé que cela..." — after adjectives/participles of belief
   "attendant que le poisson vînt" — after attendre
   Consecutive: "si grand que tout s'efface" — after intensity marker
   Comparative: "plus vite que ses moutons" — que = than
   Fixed: "bien que", "ainsi que", "de même que", "alors que", \
"tant que" — que stays SCONJ

3. ADV (restrictive) — ne...que meaning "only":
   "il ne mange que des fruits" — que = ADV (seulement)
   Key signal: "ne" earlier in the same clause.

COMMON STANZA ERRORS:

completive after a verb of speech, should be SCONJ not PRON.

relative in cleft, should be PRON.
